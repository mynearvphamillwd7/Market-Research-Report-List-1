<p><p>The SUV aluminum alloy wheel market encompasses three main types: casting, forging, and others. Casting involves pouring molten aluminum into a mold, creating intricate designs with less material waste, suitable for mass production. Forging, on the other hand, involves shaping aluminum through intense pressure, resulting in stronger and lighter wheels ideal for high-performance vehicles. The "other" category includes processes like extrusion and machining, which offer specific customizations. Each method balances cost, weight, and performance, catering to diverse consumer needs.</p></p>
