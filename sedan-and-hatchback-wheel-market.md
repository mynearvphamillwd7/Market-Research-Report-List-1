<p><p>The Sedan and Hatchback wheel market consists of two primary manufacturing processes: casting and forging. Casting involves pouring molten metal into a mold to create wheel shapes, offering flexibility in design and complex geometries, often favored for cost efficiency. In contrast, forging involves shaping metal under high pressure, resulting in stronger and lighter wheels, ideal for performance-oriented vehicles. Both methods cater to different consumer preferences, with casting typically suited for budget-friendly options and forging appealing to those seeking durability and performance.</p></p>
